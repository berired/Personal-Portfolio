# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

- Recruiters and hiring managers assessing fit and skills quickly
- Potential collaborators (other developers and designers)
- Fellow students and the creative-dev community

## Product Purpose

A retro terminal-themed personal portfolio for David Wagan, a CS student and fullstack developer at CIIT. The portfolio showcases skills, projects, and experience through an interactive CRT/terminal aesthetic. Success means a visitor immediately understands David's technical creativity, sees real work, and can reach out.

## Brand Personality

Retro, technical, handcrafted. The experience should feel like booting up a personal operating system — deliberate, atmospheric, and authored.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / Bootstrap layouts (blue-and-white cards, hero + feature grids)
- Overdesigned 3D-agency sites (WebGL as spectacle, slow loaders, style over substance)
- Bland dark-mode developer blogs with no visual identity

## Design Principles

1. **Atmosphere over decoration** — Every visual element (scanlines, glow, beam) serves the retro-terminal world. Nothing decorative for its own sake.
2. **Smooth feels real** — Camera animations and transitions should feel cinematic but fast; slow loading breaks immersion.
3. **Leave the design alone** — The aesthetic is intentional and confirmed. Changes are structural and technical (responsiveness, accessibility, performance) only.
4. **Content first on every screen** — Navigation, text, and interactive elements must remain usable at 320px as well as 4K.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA target
- Minimum 44×44px touch targets for all interactive elements
- Labels properly associated with form controls
- aria-current, aria-label, and aria-live on key UI regions
- Respect prefers-reduced-motion for animations
